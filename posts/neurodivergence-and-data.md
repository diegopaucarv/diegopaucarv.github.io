# Neurodivergence and Data Science

There is a particular kind of clarity that comes with hyperfocus. When a dataset pulls me in, the rest of the world recedes. Hours pass. Patterns emerge that I suspect a more linear thinker might have mapped differently — not better, not worse, just differently.

## The Pattern Problem

Data science is, at its core, a discipline of pattern recognition. You gather observations, model their relationships, and make inferences about structure you cannot fully see. This is also, I have come to believe, what neurodivergent minds do instinctively — often without the formal vocabulary.

The challenge is not aptitude. The challenge is translation: converting an internal, often non-linear understanding of a system into a linear, legible output that institutions can consume.

## Research as Stimming

I use the word loosely, but there is something genuinely regulatory about sustained inquiry. The act of constructing a methodology, deciding on units of analysis, iterating on a question — it imposes structure from the inside. Not the structure that is handed to you, but the structure you build yourself.

This is why I find formal methods so liberating rather than constraining. A clear protocol becomes a track I can run on.

## What I Have Learned

- Difficulty with open-ended tasks is not laziness. It is an absence of structure. Give it structure and it disappears.
- The "context-switching penalty" is real. Deep work is non-negotiable.
- Notation systems and personal knowledge graphs are not productivity hacks. They are cognitive prosthetics.

This is an ongoing conversation. I expect these notes to grow.
